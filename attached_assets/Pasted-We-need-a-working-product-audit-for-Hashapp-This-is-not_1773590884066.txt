We need a working-product audit for Hashapp.

This is not a design critique and not a feature brainstorm.
The goal is to tell us, brutally and concretely:

What is real, what is fake, what is broken, and what must become real before Hashapp counts as a working product rather than a polished prototype.

Important context
Hashapp now has:
a strong consumer-grade frontend prototype
a real Base-native spend permission proof on Base Sepolia
non-custodial product language
spend permissions as a first-class object
Money / Activity / Scout / Rules structure
Important: the Base Sepolia spend-permission proof already exists, but it was produced by a different technical Replit agent as a standalone proof artifact.

For this audit:
treat that proof as real and available
do not assume the current app is already integrated with it end-to-end
part of your job is identifying the gap between the proven primitive and the current product surface

Audit scope
Please audit these areas:

Wallet / custody truth
Does the product accurately reflect a non-custodial model?
Does any copy still imply Hashapp holds funds?
Does the wallet connection story feel real or still mostly simulated?

Permission handshake truth
What actually happens when the user taps Grant Permission?
Is permission issuance real, mocked, or partially stubbed?
Is the spend permission model represented honestly?
Does the app reflect the proven Base-native primitive accurately, or just gesture at it?

In-bounds vs out-of-bounds enforcement
Which parts of the app are backed by real enforcement?
Which parts are only simulated?
Does the app imply more than the proof actually covers?
Where is the proof real but not yet integrated into the product?

Receipt / proof truth
Which receipt/proof fields are real?
Which are mocked?
Are all proof links real?
Are failed actions clearly separated from settled actions?

Endpoint / data flow truth
Which endpoints are live?
Which are broken?
Which buttons are dead or simulated?
Where does the current product break under real usage?

Identity truth
Which identities are real?
Which ENS/Basenames are decorative only?
Does any vendor identity feel misleading or over-literal?

UX breakpoints under real plumbing
Where does real wallet or permission logic break the cinematic flow?
Which interactions work as a prototype but would collapse when real state is connected?

Output format
Please return:
A. Working product verdict
Is Hashapp currently:
polished prototype
MVP with some real internals
working product in progress
something else

B. What is real
List the flows/components that are truly grounded today.

C. What is fake or simulated
List the parts that still look real but are not yet real.

D. What is broken
List all broken endpoints, dead CTAs, invalid proof paths, or misleading states.

E. Integration gap
Given that the spend-permission proof exists separately, what exactly must happen to connect that proof honestly into the current app?

F. P0 fixes
What must become real or be removed before submission.

G. Overclaim risks
Anywhere the UI/story is claiming more than the current plumbing proves.

Important
Do not give a generic “looks good” review.
Do not suggest expanding scope.
Do not redesign the product.

We need a ruthless truth audit so we know where to focus next.
