We are no longer changing the product structure. Keep the current top-level architecture:

Money
Activity
Scout
Rules

This next pass is about product clarity and polish, not widening scope.

Priority 1 — Make spend permissions first-class
Add a visible Spend Permission concept to the product so recurring/conditional payments feel explicit, not hidden inside generic rules.

Use the DataStream Pro example:
vendor
amount
cadence
active / revoked state
clear relationship to the rule that allows or blocks it

This should strengthen the “Agents that pay” story by making conditional/revocable spend permissions legible.

Priority 2 — Tighten the Money tab’s non-custodial language
Make sure the Money tab never implies Hashapp holds user funds.

Prefer language like:
Available for Scout
Allocated to Scout
Protected by your rules
Connected wallet
Increase Scout’s spending limit
Adjust Scout’s budget

Avoid language that sounds like funds are deposited into Hashapp or stored by the app.

Priority 3 — Activity polish
Push the Activity screen further without changing its structure:
stronger hierarchy between intent, status, and amount
tighter wrapping in pending request copy
richer row rhythm
premium motion / micro-interactions
trusted destination rail should feel clearly trusted, not generic

The feed is still the hero of the product.

Priority 4 — ENS / Basename identity polish
Where helpful, use human-readable identity treatment for:
trusted destinations
Scout identity
receipt proof context
search results

This should improve trust/readability without turning ENS into a loud feature.

Priority 5 — Rules polish only
Do not redesign the Rules screen. Just make it feel less template-like:
more intentional toggle styling
slightly stronger hierarchy
more premium spacing/copy treatment

Do not do
do not add new top-level tabs
do not widen scope
do not add backend complexity
do not re-architect the app
do not turn the UI into a crypto dashboard

Goal: make the current prototype feel more inevitable, more trustworthy, and more clearly aligned with “scoped spending permissions + auditable proof.”
